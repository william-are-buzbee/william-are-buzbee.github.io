# Image Generation Prompts — Creature Visual Exploration

These are not meant to produce the final creature. They're meant to produce images where SOME element — a texture, a posture, a face quality, a mood — feels right, and you can pull that element out as directional reference. Run each prompt multiple times and cherry-pick.

Adjust the style suffix for your preferred generator. Midjourney tends to respond well to the photographic and cinematic framing. DALL-E handles the descriptive paragraphs well. Stable Diffusion / SDXL benefits from the tag-style prompts.

## CLADE A — Meso-Predator (~22 kg, six-limbed, chemical-sensing predator)

### Prompt A1 — The Face (Chemical Organ Focus)

"Close-up portrait of an alien animal's face. No visible mouth. Two wet, folded, glistening sensory organs on the front of the head where cheeks would be — exposed mucosal tissue in recessed grooves, pinkish-red against dark wrinkled grey-brown skin. The organs look like externalized nasal tissue, ridged and moist. Small eyes set to either side, angled slightly forward. On top of the head, two pairs of large mobile skin flaps — like bat ears crossed with a frill-necked lizard's frill, clearly expressive, currently splayed in an alert position. The overall face reads as attentive and alien. No fur, no scales. Thick wrinkled skin like an elephant. Warm amber side-lighting. Photorealistic creature concept art."

### Prompt A2 — The Face (Ear-Flap Expression Focus)

"Front view of an alien creature's head. The dominant features are multiple mobile flaps on top of the skull — thin, membranous, independently articulating, like oversized bat ears but wider and flatter, arranged in a crown-like spread. The flaps are mid-fold, some raised, some partially lowered, giving an impression of focused attention. Below the flaps, the face is broad and blunt with small dark eyes set wide. The skin is thick, dark, deeply wrinkled like a rhinoceros or elephant. No obvious mouth visible from this angle. Diffuse amber lighting. The creature looks intelligent and wary. Photorealistic, natural history documentary style."

### Prompt A3 — Full Body Silhouette and Stance

"Side view of a six-legged alien animal walking, roughly the size of a medium dog. Low to the ground, wide stance. The body proportions resemble a komodo dragon but with three pairs of limbs instead of two. The front pair is shorter and held closer to the body — more like arms than legs, with visible joint articulation at the wrist. The middle and rear pairs are heavier, clearly the locomotion drive. The skin is dark, thick, wrinkled — naked mole rat texture at elephant scale. The head is broad and flat, wider than it is tall, with mobile flaps on top folded back during movement. The tail is short or absent. The animal moves with a deliberate, heavy gait. Natural environment: dark reddish-brown low vegetation, amber light, damp ground. Photorealistic creature design, full body visible."

### Prompt A4 — The Body Texture and Weight

"Alien animal resting on dark wet ground, body filling the frame. Six limbs tucked partly beneath it. The focus is the skin: thick, dark grey-brown, deeply creased and wrinkled, slightly loose over heavy musculature visible beneath. The texture is between elephant hide and naked mole rat skin — no fur, no scales, no plates. Clearly soft tissue, clearly tough. Mineral dust and moisture on the skin surface. Some areas are smoother (the flanks), some deeply folded (the joints, the neck). The animal's mass is evident — it looks heavy, solid, dense. Warm low lighting. Photorealistic macro detail."

### Prompt A5 — Predatory Moment (Front Limb Kill, Not Bite)

"An alien six-limbed predator pinning prey with its front pair of limbs. The front limbs are shorter than the others, with thick curved claws gripping down. The body is low, weight forward, the rear four limbs braced wide for stability. The head is tilted slightly downward — the mouth is on the underside of the head, not visible from this angle. Thick dark wrinkled skin, heavy build. The scene reads as powerful and controlled, not frantic. Like a raptor pinning a rabbit — the talons do the work, the head comes after. Dark ground, amber atmospheric lighting. Photorealistic creature concept art."

### Prompt A6 — The Herbivore Variant (Large, Barrel-Bodied, ~200 kg)

"A massive six-limbed alien herbivore standing in shallow mineral-tinted water. Barrel-shaped body, very heavy, low to the ground. Front limbs are broad and flat — paddle-like, used for digging and swimming. Rear limbs are thick columns. The skin is extremely thick, wrinkled, slightly oily or mucous-coated for water resistance — darker and heavier than any Earth animal's hide. The head is proportionally small compared to the enormous torso, with mobile flaps on top lying flat and relaxed. The eyes are on the sides of the head, wide-set, giving a panoramic alert expression. No visible mouth from this angle — it's underneath, pointing down for grazing substrate. The animal looks placid, massive, and armored by sheer thickness rather than shell. Turquoise mineral deposits on nearby rocks. Warm diffuse amber light. Photorealistic, natural history style."

## CLADE B — Ambush Predator (~24 kg, eight-limbed, vibration-sensing predator)

### Prompt B1 — The Face (Eyes and Mouthparts)

"Close-up of an alien creature's face. Four dark, glossy, highly reflective eyes arranged in a diamond pattern — two large ones on top, two smaller below. The eyes have a jumping spider quality: clearly focused, clearly watching, with visible depth and attention in the reflection. Below the eyes, complex multi-part mouthparts — interlocking structures that look precise and mechanical, like crab maxillipeds but denser and more intricate. Currently still, but clearly capable of rapid independent movement. The surrounding integument is smooth, layered, semi-translucent — like polished bone or thick fingernail material, with a dull semi-gloss surface. Not chitin, not skin. A material between shell and horn. The face gives nothing away emotionally. It is simply watching. Dark background, single cool light source. Photorealistic creature concept art."

### Prompt B2 — The Face (Unreadable Attention)

"Front portrait of an alien animal with four dark reflective eyes in a diamond arrangement on a smooth, featureless face. The integument is pale grey-brown, slightly translucent, with visible layered structure beneath the surface like cuttlefish bone. No expression. No readable emotion. The eyes are the only feature that communicates, and what they communicate is attention — steady, patient, focused. Below the eyes, something complex and folded is visible but still. The creature looks like it is deciding something but giving no indication what. Moody low lighting, shallow depth of field on the eyes. Photorealistic, unsettling, naturalistic."

### Prompt B3 — Full Body Showing Limb Differentiation

"Side view of an eight-limbed alien creature the size of a medium dog. The body is compact, continuously curved, with smooth overlapping segments — like a giant isopod or a pillbug but more streamlined and less obviously segmented. No dramatic waist or pinching between sections. The integument is smooth, layered, with a dull semi-gloss like horseshoe crab shell. The rear four limbs are thick, planted, clearly load-bearing — the locomotion base. The front four limbs are longer, thinner, and each is doing something different: one touching the ground, one reaching forward, one angled to the side, one curled back. They move independently like octopus arms. Four dark eyes visible at the front, low to the body line. The animal is still but looks ready to move in any direction. Dark ground, natural low lighting. Photorealistic creature design, full body."

### Prompt B4 — The Integument Texture

"Extreme close-up of alien animal skin. Not chitin, not mammal skin, not reptile scales. A semi-flexible layered material — like cuttlefish bone crossed with thick fingernail or horn. Smooth surface with a dull semi-gloss. Slightly translucent in thin areas — you can faintly see darker structures beneath the surface. The layers are visible at the edges where segments overlap, like growth rings in horn material. The color is pale grey-brown to cream, with subtle mineral-tinted variation. Where the material is thickest it's opaque and smooth. Where it's thinnest it's faintly see-through and you can sense movement or color beneath it. Macro photography, shallow depth of field. Photorealistic texture study."

### Prompt B5 — The Independent Limbs

"Four alien limbs emerging from the front of a compact arthropod-like body, each doing something completely different. One limb is pressed flat against a rock surface, sensing vibration. One is extended forward, tip touching the air. One is curled inward, manipulating something small. One is angled sideways, braced against a wall. Each limb is thin, articulated, covered in the same smooth layered integument as the body but thinner — you can see internal structures faintly through the translucent material. Small sensory structures are visible along the limb surfaces — rows of tiny raised bumps or pores. The limbs look autonomous, like each one is controlled by its own mind. The body they emerge from is visible but out of focus — smooth, compact, low to the ground. Dark environment, single light source. Photorealistic creature concept art."

### Prompt B6 — The Ambush Moment (Still, Watching, Territory)

"An alien eight-limbed creature pressed flat against a dark rock wall in a cave environment. The body is compact and curved, hugging the surface. The integument matches the rock closely in color — pale mineral grey-brown. Four dark reflective eyes are the only feature that stands out, catching faint light. The front four limbs are spread wide against the rock, pressed flat, sensing. The rear four limbs are coiled beneath the body, ready to spring. The creature is perfectly still. It has been still for a long time. The scene is quiet, tense, and the creature is the most dangerous thing in it — but you might not see it immediately. Dark cave environment, faint ambient light, mineral-encrusted surfaces. Photorealistic, atmospheric, predatory stillness."

## MIXED — Both Creatures for Contrast

### Prompt C1 — Two Different Alien Animals in the Same Environment

"Two alien animals in the same dark, amber-lit wetland environment, clearly from different lineages. On the left: a six-limbed creature with thick dark wrinkled skin like an elephant, a broad head with mobile flaps on top, small eyes, and a heavy low stance — warm, muscular, expressive. On the right: an eight-limbed creature with smooth pale layered integument like polished bone, four dark reflective eyes, thin independent front limbs each doing something different — cool, precise, unreadable. Both are roughly medium-dog sized. They are aware of each other but not interacting. The contrast is the point: one looks like an animal, the other looks like a presence. Dark reddish-brown vegetation, amber atmospheric light, wet ground. Photorealistic creature concept art, natural history illustration quality."

## NOTES FOR GENERATOR USE

- If results are too "sci-fi monster," add: "naturalistic, believable anatomy, creature designed by evolution not by an artist, David Attenborough documentary aesthetic"
- If results are too insectoid for Clade A, add: "mammalian weight and musculature, soft thick skin, no exoskeleton, no chitin, no compound eyes"
- If results are too mammalian for Clade B, add: "not fur, not mammal, smooth layered integument, no facial expression, multiple eyes, independent limb movement"
- If results are too symmetrical/posed, add: "caught mid-movement, asymmetric limb positions, candid wildlife photography composition"
- For Midjourney specifically, appending "--ar 16:9 --style raw --s 200" tends to reduce the fantasy-illustration tendency and push toward naturalistic rendering
- For DALL-E, the paragraph-style prompts above should work as-is
- For Stable Diffusion, you may want to extract key tags: "alien creature, six limbs, thick wrinkled skin, no fur, no scales, wide-set eyes, mobile ear flaps, low stance, amber lighting, photorealistic"
