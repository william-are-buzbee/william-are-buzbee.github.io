# Overworld - Alien Ecology Simulation

A tile-based browser game about survival on a believably simulated alien world. 

The environments, flora and fauna of this planet have evolved according to their selective pressures. The dense atmosphere, dim sunlight and moody atmosphere characterize the planet's surface- swampy marshlands covered in mahogany-red trees. The strong presence of metals like manganese and copper in the planet's core provide a nearly inexhaustible supply of energy for chemosynthesising life, creating niches of flora that rival traditional photosynthesisers. 

This mixture of two distinct strategies is mirrored in the evolution of large fauna on this planet. All non-microbial life can be traced back to two ancient lineages: a clade that senses the world through smell and has a central brain and another that senses using vibrations and has a brain distributed evenly across its whole body. 

Over millions of years, these two clades radiate into every niche available. They evolve into various roles in the competition for calories, from grazing herbivores and apex predators to aquatic behemoths and tiny generalists. Their expanse touches every biome and corner of the planet.

The game begins now, after these millions of years have passed. You play as an alien creature just trying to survive. Learn the ecology of the planet and how its inhabitants think. Use your animal weaponry and senses to survive. Navigate a web of ecological interactions. Discover a unique strategy for survival on this alien world. 


This game is inspired by Biblaridion's _Alien Biospheres_ series, an intelligent speculative evolution project. Other strong influences come from classic roguelikes like _Nethack_ and _Caves of Qud._
