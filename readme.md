# Overworld - Alien Ecology Simulation

A tile-based browser game about survival on a believably simulated alien world. 

---------

The dense atmosphere, dim sunlight and moist conditions of this planet have created a wet and rich surface habitat- swampy marshlands covered in mahogany-red flora. Metals like manganese and copper are abundant in the planetary core, providing a nearly inexhaustible supply of energy for chemosynthetic life. 

This mixture of photosynthesis and chemosynthesis is mirrored by the indigenous fauna of the planet- all creatures can be traced back to two ancient lineages. These two clades are completely distinct.

Over millions of years, these two lineages radiate into every possible niche. They come to occupy the roles of apex predators, tiny generalists, titanic herbivores and carrion eating scavengers. Their expanse touches every biome and corner of the planet, from the deepest corners of the oceans to the vast sub-terranian complexes beneath the surface. 

This is when the game begins. You play as an alien creature trying to survive in this world. Learn the characteristics of the planet and how its inhabitants operate. Navigate a web of ecological interaction. Use your animal weaponry, senses and cognition to survive. Learn how to fight and how to live another day. 

---------

This game is inspired by Biblaridion's _Alien Biospheres_ series, an intelligent speculative evolution project. Other strong influences come from classic roguelikes like _Nethack_ and _Caves of Qud._
